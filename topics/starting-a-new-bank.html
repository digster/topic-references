Starting a New Bank

Last updated: 2026-06-23

"Starting a bank" today splits into two very different paths. You can charter a brand-new bank — a de novo bank that holds its own license, takes deposits and makes loans (a slow, capital-heavy process) — or you can launch a neobank or embedded-finance product on top of an existing licensed "sponsor" bank using Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS), which is how most modern fintech "banks" actually get off the ground.

Either way the real work is the same business underneath: gathering deposits cheaply, lending or investing them profitably (the net interest margin), moving money safely, and earning fees — while staying trustworthy. Understanding that business, the API building blocks that now let you assemble it, and where a real banking license still matters is what separates a durable bank from a thin app sitting on someone else's rails.

Good to know first: a little balance-sheet literacy (assets vs. liabilities, deposits vs. loans) and a rough mental model of how card payments move money will make everything below click faster — but neither is required.

📚 Books

  • Bank 4.0: Banking Everywhere, Never at a Bank — Brett King, Wiley (2018) Beginner

    The big-picture case for why banking is becoming embedded in technology rather than branches — the worldview behind every neobank.

  • Digital Bank: Strategies to Launch or Become a Digital Bank — Chris Skinner (2014) Intermediate

    Exactly what the title says: a strategy-level playbook for standing up a digital bank, with real challenger-bank case studies.

  • The Anatomy of the Swipe: Making Money Move — Ahmed Siddiqui (2020) Intermediate

    A clear, builder-focused tour of how card payments and money movement actually work — essential plumbing if you'll issue accounts or cards.

📄 Papers & Primary References

  • Applying for Deposit Insurance — A Handbook for Organizers of De Novo Institutions — FDIC Intermediate

    The FDIC's own plain-language guide to the steps of forming a brand-new U.S. bank — the one regulatory document worth skimming if you take the full-charter route.

✍️ Articles & Blog Posts

  • Every Company Will Be a Fintech Company — Angela Strange, Andreessen Horowitz (2020) Beginner

    The influential thesis that financial infrastructure is now available "as a service," letting almost any company embed banking — the backdrop for today's neobanks.

  • A Fintech Guide to Bank Partners and Sponsors — Lithic Intermediate

    Practical walkthrough of the most common path to "a bank" today: finding, vetting and working with a sponsor bank, including the three partnership models.

🎥 Videos & Talks

  • What to Expect in Fintech's Next 10 Years — Angela Strange, fintech_devcon (2021) Intermediate

    The case that open financial infrastructure lets startups launch banking products without becoming a bank — and what gets built next.

  • Bank on It — Financial Service Firm Valuation — Aswath Damodaran, NYU Stern Advanced

    NYU's renowned valuation professor on what actually drives a bank's economics and value — the rigorous "bank as a business" lens.

🎓 Courses & Tutorials

  • Banking & Money — Khan Academy (free) Beginner

    Short, free videos that build the fundamentals from scratch: what a bank does, balance sheets, and how lending creates money.

  • FinTech: Foundations & Applications of Financial Technology — Wharton / University of Pennsylvania, Coursera Intermediate

    A four-course series on payments, lending, regulation and the technologies reshaping financial services (auditable for free).

🔧 Tools & Interactive

  • Unit — Embedded-finance platform

    Spin up accounts, cards and money movement via API on top of partner banks — a concrete look at what BaaS gives you out of the box.

  • Treasury Prime — Embedded banking / BaaS

    Connects fintechs to a network of sponsor banks through one API — useful for seeing how the bank-partner layer is packaged.

  • Stripe Treasury — Banking-as-a-service by Stripe

    Lets platforms embed FDIC-insured accounts, cards and payouts for their own customers — the productized version of "a bank as an API."

  • Marqeta — Modern card issuing

    The card-issuing layer behind many fintech debit/credit programs — shows how cards, controls and authorizations are built.

  • Plaid — Financial data connectivity

    Connects apps to users' existing bank accounts for verification, balances and transfers — a staple of the modern fintech stack.

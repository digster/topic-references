Transformers (Deep Learning)

Last updated: 2026-06-22

The Transformer is a neural network architecture, introduced in 2017, that processes sequences (like text) using a mechanism called self-attention rather than the recurrence or convolutions used by earlier models. Self-attention lets every token in a sequence directly weigh the relevance of every other token, which makes the architecture highly parallelizable and very effective at modeling long-range context.

Transformers are the backbone of nearly all modern large language models (GPT, BERT, Llama, Claude, and more) and have since spread far beyond text — into vision, audio, code, and even protein folding. Understanding them is the single highest-leverage topic for making sense of today's AI systems.

Good to know first: basic neural networks and backpropagation, a little linear algebra (vectors, matrices, dot products), and some Python/PyTorch if you want to follow the hands-on resources.

  • Speech and Language Processing (3rd ed., draft) — Dan Jurafsky & James H. Martin Intermediate

    Free, authoritative NLP textbook with clear, math-grounded chapters on transformers and LLMs.

📄 Papers & Primary References

    The original paper that introduced the Transformer. Worth reading once you have the intuition from the explainers below.
